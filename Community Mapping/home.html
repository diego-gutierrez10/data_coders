    STARLESS

    Starless is a research project on light pollution in Mexico City, which we have observed to have increased over the past three years. In this research, we propose a media escalation called Starless and suggest ideas to reduce this pollution, based on the proposals of San Pedro Martín.

    About

    Light pollution in Mexico is an increasing problem, especially in large cities like Mexico City. This type of pollution refers to the excessive emission of artificial light in areas where it is not necessary, interfering with the natural environment and affecting both human well-being and ecosystems. In recent years, rapid urbanization and a lack of proper regulation have contributed to this phenomenon.

    Light pollution has negative effects on ecosystems. Nocturnal animals such as bats, insects, and birds have their natural behavioral patterns disrupted due to exposure to artificial light. This affects their ability to navigate, find food, and reproduce, directly impacting biodiversity.

    Prolonged exposure to artificial light, particularly blue light from high-intensity LED lights, can disrupt circadian rhythms in humans, leading to sleep disorders, fatigue, and related health problems. Additionally, the lack of proper darkness in urban nights can increase levels of stress and anxiety.

    Mexico has a rich tradition in astronomy, and light pollution affects the visibility of the night sky, interfering with both professional and recreational stargazing. Areas that were once ideal for cosmic observation are now limited by artificial brightness.

    The lack of proper urban planning has allowed public lighting and building illumination to be inefficient and unsustainable. This has led to increased light pollution in urban and suburban areas.

    Some states have begun implementing regulations to control the use of artificial light. For example, the Mexican Official Standard NOM-025-STPS-2008 regulates lighting levels in workplaces. However, a broader and more specific legal framework on light pollution in public and residential areas is still needed.

    San Pedro Martir

    San Pedro Mártir, a locality near Ensenada, Baja California, has gained recognition for its efforts to combat light pollution, particularly due to its proximity to the San Pedro Mártir National Park and the Observatorio Astronómico Nacional.

    One of the primary proposals is the installation of shielded lighting fixtures that direct light downward rather than allowing it to scatter into the sky. This measure aims to minimize the upward spread of artificial light, preserving the natural darkness essential for astronomical observation​
